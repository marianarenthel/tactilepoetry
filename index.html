I am un-effective with this work.
Everything I do ends up somewhat crooked,
uneven
and execution time is so slow
in my hands that time slips out
and becomes excruciating.
My back hurts,
I end up frustrated with wasting time
there is no contemplation
or extatic enlightenment but something else.
I try,
without proclaiming that this is something,
not even art;
but lately,
this is what my hands produce comfortably, without purpose
alone.
 
On Tactile Poetry
 
Using the textile medium in order to write graphically and materially, expressing abstract ideas and information within ancient civilizations has been a well known fact among ethnographers and archaeologists . As an example, the use of Quipus (in the Inca empire) as a piece of writing was frequently used for such a purpose. The beautiful and synthetic knotting systems within the manufacture of all textile pieces whether for functional or ornamental objects, was vastly used for the transmission of some vital information regarding the environment, agriculture, and even time. The search for hints in the realm of notation in textile material for poetic and aesthetic purposes could lead to an aspect of the mechanics for a so called contemporary art production . Technical mechanisms and ways of making have tended to be depreciated by the idea that their only value lies in automatist learnt movements, non consequent repetition for acquiring copying techniques for reproduction or learning processes. The importance of reconsidering certain technical ways, practices and mannerism opens up to enlighten a sort of unregulated system that involves creation and notation in the realm of material manipulation. It is interesting to have an approach to investigation based in arts practice field so as to confront the notion of a visually (and conceptually) centred art system that could also succumb to the tactile notion of material and all of it’s spectrum of qualities. This subject matter has been a source of enquirer for a couple of years, taking the form of large pieces that were produced with a knitting machine from the mid 70’s. The intention is to keep exploring for different ways of graphic grammatical constructions as also materials and surfaces, to the extent of expanding experience towards materiality and storytelling; tracing a bridge between the past artifacts and sensible experience.
